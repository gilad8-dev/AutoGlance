## Design Context

### Users
Regular everyday people using the browser. Not developers, not power users — they should feel the interface is *intuitive* without being told how to use it. Common context: mid-task on a webpage, wants help from the AI without losing their place. The side panel sits next to real content, so it must not visually fight that content.

### Brand Personality
**Warm. Aesthetic. Vibrant.** Friendly and confident without being cute. Considered, not corporate. It should feel like a well-designed object — something with taste — rather than a "software tool." Reference admired: Anthropic's design language (warm creams, humanist serifs, quiet vibrance, tinted dark mode). But this is AutoGlance — it has its own voice, not a knockoff.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: follow system preference (`prefers-color-scheme`). Warm cream in light, warm near-black in dark — never pure white or pure black, never cold slate.
- **Palette**: warm neutrals tinted toward the brand hue + a vibrant warm accent (persimmon / coral-orange family, NOT indigo-purple).
- **Typography**: a humanist serif for display moments (logo, welcome, headings in assistant bubbles) paired with a warm sans for body/UI. System fallbacks acceptable — we'll bundle WOFF2 to hit the real look.
- **Motion**: restrained, smooth, ease-out-quart for state changes. No bouncing or elastic.
- **Hard anti-references**: ChatGPT-style lavender gradients, Notion AI purple, generic indigo → violet bubbles, pure-white cards with blue-hue shadows.

### Design Principles
1. **Warm before clever** — every neutral tint points toward the accent hue, so nothing feels "sterile UI."
2. **Let the content breathe** — chat is mostly text; reduce chrome, use rhythm and typography to do the work instead of cards and borders.
3. **Intentional vibrance** — one saturated accent, used rarely. A coral send button reads louder than five colored chips.
4. **Human, not technical** — warmth comes through humanist letterforms and a visible serif moment, not through emoji or rounded-square icons.
5. **Symmetrical light and dark** — dark mode is not "light mode with colors inverted." It's a separate warm palette designed for late-night reading.
